# Vision

## Why This App Exists

I take a lot of photos and want a beautiful, private place to share them with people close to me — without the noise, algorithms, and public exposure of Instagram or Facebook. Existing platforms optimize for engagement, not for the craft of photography.

## Who It's For

- **Me** (the photographer) — to publish and organize my work
- **Friends and family** — invited personally, to view, react, and stay connected to what I create

## What It Is

A private, invite-only photo community for a small group of people. I publish photos organized into galleries and photo walk stories. Friends can browse, like, comment, and message me. It feels more like a personal photography site with a social layer than a social media platform.

## Goals

- Photos look great — the UI serves the images, not the other way around
- Only people I invite can access the app
- Simple and fast to use on both desktop and mobile
- I can organize photos into galleries, collections, and photo walk stories
- Friends can react and comment without friction

## Non-Goals

- Not a public platform — no SEO, no discovery, no public profiles
- Not a professional portfolio site (no client inquiries, no licensing)
- Not built for large scale — designed for tens of users, not thousands
- No native mobile app (responsive web is enough for now)

## Core Principles

- **Photos first** — every UI decision should make photos look better, never worse
- **Private by default** — access is always gated by invitation
- **Simple over feature-rich** — build less, but build it well
